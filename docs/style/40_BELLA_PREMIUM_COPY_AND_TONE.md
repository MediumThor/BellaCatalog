# Bella Premium Copy and Tone Guide

## Goal

Define the product voice for premium customer-facing BellaCatalog experiences.

This guide applies especially to Layout Studio, but should also be usable later for broader product refactors.

---

## Tone attributes

The product voice should feel:

- confident
- calm
- polished
- clear
- elevated
- modern
- helpful
- never chatty
- never robotic
- never technical without reason

---

## Writing principles

### 1. Be concise
Do not over-explain obvious actions.

### 2. Sound premium
Use restrained, confident language.

### 3. Guide the next step
Good copy should orient the user.

### 4. Avoid unnecessary jargon
Especially in customer-facing views.

### 5. Keep warnings calm
Warnings should be constructive, not alarmist.

---

## Good tone examples

Good:
- Upload a plan to begin your layout.
- Calibrate one known dimension to size your pieces accurately.
- Place your pieces on the slab to preview coverage.
- A few pieces still need placement.
- Layout saved.
- This option appears to require two slabs.

Avoid:
- Please select a valid file for ingestion.
- Geometry invalid.
- Fatal layout mismatch.
- Missing required object.
- Operation completed successfully.

---

## Labeling guidance

Prefer:
- human-readable labels
- polished action names
- short clear section titles

Examples:
- Layout Studio
- Visual Layout
- Piece Details
- Slab Preview
- Saved just now
- Not yet placed

Avoid unnecessarily technical terms unless they are truly useful.

---

## Warning copy style

Warnings should:
- state the issue clearly
- suggest the next step
- avoid panic language

Examples:
- Scale has not been set yet.
- One or more pieces extend beyond the slab bounds.
- Not all pieces have been placed.
- Add a known dimension to improve accuracy.

---

## Empty state tone

Empty states should feel intentional and premium.

Good empty state pattern:
- one-line title
- one or two lines of calm explanation
- one obvious action

---

## Customer-facing polish

Because this feature may be used in front of customers, copy should never feel:
- unfinished
- developer-oriented
- crude
- noisy
- overly technical

The software should present itself like a high-end sales tool.
